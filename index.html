How it works

A simple teacher workflow. No complex setup. Designed for quick use.

1
Add the submission

Upload a document or paste text. Retensiq uses the actual content to build questions.

2
Assign a short check

Choose Text or Voice mode. Configure number of questions and time expectations.

3
Review signals + summary

See answers plus timing/fluency indicators that help you gauge comprehension.

Signal examples (high-level)
  • Response timing: time to first input and time to complete.
  • Consistency: whether answers align with the submitted content.
  • Voice cadence: fluency and natural explanation vs scripted reading.
Retensiq provides signals to support teacher judgment; it is not a disciplinary “verdict engine.”

Two modes: Text or Voice

Teachers choose the best fit for the moment and the student.

Text mode (sequential questions)
Fast + focused
  • One question at a time to reduce copy/paste behavior.
  • Captures time-to-first-input and completion time.
  • Mix of factual, conceptual, and opinion/defense questions.
Voice mode (spoken responses)
More human
  • Students explain in their own words; cadence and fluency are informative.
  • Reduces the “type it into another tool” loophole.
  • Teacher review includes transcript + high-level speaking indicators.

FAQ

Does Retensiq prove AI was used?

No. Retensiq helps validate whether the student understands the content they submitted. It supports teacher judgment with grounded questions and response signals.

Is this a grade?

No. It’s designed as a short integrity and comprehension check to inform next steps, not replace grading.

How long does it take?

Typically 3–7 minutes depending on configuration. It’s meant to be quick enough to use when suspicion arises.

What does the student experience look like?

A focused, sequential flow: one question at a time, with either typed responses or voice answers. Minimal distraction, maximum clarity.
